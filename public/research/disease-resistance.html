Natural Resistance Through Selective Breeding

KSSEED focuses on developing vegetable varieties with inherent resistance to the most common pests and diseases that threaten Cambodian agriculture. Rather than relying on chemical inputs, we identify and breed natural resistance traits that allow crops to defend themselves. This approach reduces pesticide costs for farmers, protects soil health, and produces safer, healthier vegetables for Cambodian consumers and export markets.

🌿 100% natural breeding — no genetic modification involved
🧪 Laboratory-confirmed resistance testing before variety release
👨‍🌾 On-farm validation with Cambodian farmers across multiple provinces
📉 Proven to cut pesticide use by up to 60% in field trials

Our Resistance Development Process

From identifying threats to releasing certified resistant varieties — a rigorous 4-stage journey.

🔬 1

Disease Identification

We map and analyze the major pests, viruses, fungi, and bacteria affecting vegetable crops across all provinces of Cambodia.

🔍 2

Resistance Screening

We screen wild relatives and traditional varieties using genomic tools to identify natural resistance genes and valuable defense traits.

🌱 3

Breeding & Selection

Through controlled crosses and multi-generation selection, we combine resistance with high yield, good taste, and desirable farmer traits.

📋 4

Evaluation & Release

New varieties undergo rigorous on-farm trials with farmer participation before being certified and released to Cambodian farmers.

Resistance Against Major Threats

Our varieties are engineered to fight back against four categories of biological threats common in Cambodia.

Featured
🦠

Viral Disease Resistance

We breed varieties with strong resistance to the most destructive plant viruses in Cambodia, including Cucumber Mosaic Virus (CMV), Papaya Ringspot Virus (PRSV), Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV), and Pepper Mottle Virus. These viruses can cause 40–80% yield losses if uncontrolled.

CMV PRSV TYLCV Pepper Mottle
🍄

Fungal Disease Resistance

Resistance to powdery mildew, anthracnose, leaf blight, and downy mildew — the most yield-damaging fungal threats in Cambodia's humid climate.

Powdery Mildew Anthracnose
🐛

Insect Pest Tolerance

Varieties with natural tolerance to whiteflies, aphids, spider mites, and thrips — vectors that spread viruses and directly damage crops.

Whitefly Aphids Spider Mites
🌾

Bacterial Wilt Resistance

Varieties tolerant of bacterial wilt and soft rot — bacterial infections that can devastate an entire crop within days during Cambodia's wet season.

Bacterial Wilt Soft Rot

Why Disease-Resistant Seeds Matter

The benefits go far beyond the farm — from healthier food to a cleaner environment.

💰

Lower Production Costs

Disease-resistant varieties cut pesticide and fungicide spending by up to 60%, directly increasing farmer net income and profitability season after season.

🥦

Healthier Vegetables

With fewer chemical treatments, vegetables grown from resistant varieties carry less pesticide residue — delivering safer, better-tasting produce to Cambodian markets.

🌱

Healthier Soil

Reduced pesticide and fungicide applications protect beneficial soil microbes and earthworms, maintaining long-term soil fertility and structure for future crops.

📊

More Stable Yields

Resistant varieties maintain strong yields even during disease outbreaks and pest pressure, giving farmers reliable income throughout the growing season.

🌍

Environmental Protection

Fewer chemical inputs means less run-off into Cambodia's waterways, protecting aquatic life and the ecosystems that farming communities depend on.

⏱️

Less Labor & Stress

Farmers spend less time scouting for disease and applying treatments, freeing up labor for other farm activities and reducing the physical and financial burden of crop protection.
